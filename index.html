PhD project

Title: Legal gender equality and government revenues

Supervisor: Gr√©goire Rota-Graziosi (CERDI, UCA)

The thesis combines cross-country panel data on gendered laws with fiscal and macroeconomic indicators to quantify how removing legal barriers for women influences tax effort, tax composition and state capacity in developing and emerging economies.

Research

Ongoing projects on gender equality before the law, tax capacity, and the fiscal consequences of closing legal gaps.

Research interests

  • Legal gender equality and economic development
  • Tax capacity, tax gaps and domestic resource mobilisation
  • Political economy of fiscal reforms in low- and middle-income countries
  • Institutions, rights, and state-building

Methods & tools

Empirical work relies on cross-country panel datasets, legal indices of gender equality, and tax performance indicators. I use econometric techniques suited for policy reforms, including panel regressions, event-study designs and robustness checks to institutional heterogeneity.
